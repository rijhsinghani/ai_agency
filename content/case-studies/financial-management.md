# Weekly Financial Intelligence on Autopilot

**Category:** Financial management
**Result:** 9 automated agents cut weekly money management from hours to a 5-minute Monday morning review

## The problem

Managing finances across multiple accounts — bank, brokerage, real estate, retirement — means data lives in 4 or 5 different places with no single view. Most business owners either pay a bookkeeper to manually reconcile everything each month, or they let it pile up and deal with it at tax time. Both options cost money, and neither one helps you make better decisions during the year.

The real gap is not tracking — it is analysis. Knowing you have $12,000 sitting in a checking account is not the same as knowing what to do with it.

## What I built

I built a system that consolidates data from multiple account types — checking, credit cards, invoicing, investments — and runs 9 specialized agents every Monday at 8 AM. Each agent has one job: bookkeeping, tax strategy, debt paydown, cash allocation, investment contributions, credit card optimization, deduction finding, asset reconciliation, or orchestration. The orchestrator agent consolidates every recommendation into a single prioritized email sorted by urgency: do today, do this week, do when you have time, and send to your CPA.

Journal entries are drafted automatically and held for approval. Nothing posts without a human sign-off.

## The result

- Weekly financial review reduced to under 5 minutes — open email, act on what matters
- Automated journal entry drafts eliminate manual bookkeeping for recurring transactions
- Deduction opportunities surfaced proactively instead of discovered at year-end
- Cash surplus deployment recommendations prevent idle cash sitting at 0.01% APY
- CPA-ready summary generated weekly, replacing a monthly manual data-gathering exercise

## Why this matters for your business

If you run a business with multiple income streams, investments, or any complexity beyond a single bank account and Stripe, you are probably leaving money on the table — not through bad decisions, but through decisions that never get made. This kind of system does not replace your accountant. It makes every conversation with your accountant faster and more useful.
