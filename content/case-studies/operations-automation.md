# From Spreadsheets to a System: Running a Photography Business Like a Studio

**Category:** Operations
**Result:** 18 connected services replaced a stack of manual tools, spreadsheets, and back-and-forth coordination across a full photography and video business.

## The problem

Running a wedding photography and videography business means managing clients, photographers, videographers, bookings, payments, deliverables, and galleries — all at once. Most studios cobble this together with spreadsheets, email threads, and disconnected tools. The result is time lost to coordination that should go toward shooting and editing.

The specific pain: there was no single place where a booking connected to a photographer assignment, a payment, a photo pipeline, and a client gallery. Every handoff was manual.

## What I built

I built a unified operations system with 14 purpose-built services, all connected to a single database. When a client books through the checkout, it flows automatically into staff assignments, payment tracking, and a 6-stage photo processing pipeline. Finished photos land in a branded client gallery without anyone moving files manually. A separate 11-agent video pipeline handles editing stages in sequence, with each stage triggering the next.

The admin dashboard gives a real-time view of every active job — who is assigned, where each deliverable is in the pipeline, and what the client has accessed.

## The result

- 18 services replaced what previously required 4 separate tools and manual coordination between each
- 6-stage photo pipeline runs automatically from RAW files to client gallery delivery
- 11-stage video pipeline handles editing stages in sequence with no manual handoffs between steps
- Client portal, photographer portal, and admin dashboard all read from the same data — no duplication, no sync errors
- Stripe payments, Google Cloud Storage, and Supabase connected in one system, eliminating copy-paste between platforms

## Why this matters for your business

If your business runs on a mix of tools that don't talk to each other, every job creates coordination overhead. The time spent tracking what's done, what's pending, and who has what adds up to hours per week. A connected system doesn't just save time — it removes the category of errors that come from humans bridging gaps between disconnected software.
