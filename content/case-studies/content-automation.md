# Content Creation That Runs on What You're Already Building

**Category:** Content
**Result:** A self-learning content system that monitors development activity, generates multi-platform drafts in your voice, and targets less than 30 minutes per week of input by month 6.

## The problem

Business owners who are actively building and improving their products have the most interesting things to say — and the least time to say them. The gap between "I just automated something interesting" and "I published a post about it" is large enough that most content never gets made.

The alternative most people reach for is batch content creation: carve out a Saturday, produce 10 posts, schedule them, repeat next month. It's disconnected from what's actually happening in the business, and it shows.

## What I built

I built a content engine that monitors my product updates for meaningful changes — new features, automation improvements, workflow updates — and surfaces them as ranked content opportunities. When I approve a recommendation, the system generates talking points for a short video recording, plus written drafts for Instagram, Twitter, a blog post, and an email newsletter, all formatted for each platform.

The system learns from transcriptions of my recordings over time, improving how closely the written drafts match how I actually speak and write. As it improves, I spend less time reviewing — the goal is 90% of drafts approved without revision by month 12.

## The result

- Content recommendations surface automatically from development activity — no manual content calendar required
- 5 platform formats generated per approved topic: Instagram, Twitter, YouTube, blog, and email
- 6-8 pieces per week at 30 minutes of input by month 6, down from hours per session
- Voice learning loop improves draft quality over time using transcriptions of recorded video
- Revenue attribution tracking connects content engagement back to bookings and inquiries

## Why this matters for your business

The best content you can publish is about what you actually do. If you're improving your business, automating your processes, or solving real problems, that's material worth sharing — and it positions you as someone worth hiring. The barrier isn't ideas. It's the time between having something worth saying and getting it into a format that reaches people. A system that does that work between your decisions and your audience removes the barrier without removing your voice.
