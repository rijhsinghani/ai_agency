# Automated Client Communication That Doesn't Sound Automated

**Category:** Lead management
**Result:** 11 branded email templates, timed across 4 touchpoints, replaced manual follow-up for every booking type — with zero client emails falling through the cracks.

## The problem

When a client books a wedding photographer or videographer, they expect communication to start immediately and stay consistent until their event. In practice, most studios send a confirmation email, then go quiet until something comes up. Clients fill that silence with questions, and studios fill their inboxes answering the same ones over and over.

The problem gets worse when you offer multiple service types. Sending the wrong follow-up — a photo-only email to a cinema client — signals disorganization at exactly the moment clients need confidence.

## What I built

I built an event-driven email system that triggers the right email sequence based on which service a client booked. There are 5 distinct welcome emails (one per service combination), a logistics confirmation that goes out 30 days before the event, a week-before preparation email, and delivery notifications when media is ready. Every email is timed off the booking date and event date automatically.

The system also parses client email replies using AI. When a client replies with a venue change or ceremony time update, the system extracts the relevant detail, updates the database, and flags it for review — without the client needing to fill out a new form.

## The result

- 11 email templates covering every service type and communication stage
- 0 manual sends required for the core 3-email pre-event journey per booking
- 4 delivery notification variants matched to service type (photo, video, cinema, photo-video)
- Client replies parsed automatically — venue and time changes logged to the database without manual data entry
- Every email send logged with status tracking, so nothing goes undelivered silently

## Why this matters for your business

Most businesses underestimate the cost of inconsistent client communication. Unanswered questions create doubt. Follow-ups sent at the wrong time feel generic. An automated sequence built around your actual service workflow communicates professionalism before the client has seen a single deliverable. The difference between a system that sends emails and one that sends the right email at the right time is whether clients feel like a priority or an afterthought.
