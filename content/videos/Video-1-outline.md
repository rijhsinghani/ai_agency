## Section 1 — Hook (0-10 seconds)

**Problem statement:**
A potential client called while Sameer was on a shoot. Nobody answered. By the time he called back, they had booked someone else.

**Opening line:**
"I used to lose jobs. Not because my work wasn't good. Because a potential client called while I was shooting and nobody picked up. By the time I called back, they'd booked someone else. I was frustrated. So I built something that would text them back immediately, explain why I couldn't answer, and ask for their budget and date. I recovered 3 jobs in the first month. Then I realized every small business owner had a version of the same problem."

**Notes:**
Deliver this straight to camera. No pausing to think. Memorize and practice this exact line — it runs continuously from problem to first result to pivot. Pace is steady, not rushed. End on "version of the same problem" with a slight pause before cutting to the context section.

---

## Section 2 — Context (10-30 seconds)

**Who this affects:**
Any service business owner who takes calls while they are working — photographers, plumbers, contractors, HVAC techs. If you are on-site, you cannot answer the phone, and the person who called will call the next person on Google.

**Why it matters:**
The window to respond to an inbound inquiry is under 5 minutes before the prospect moves on. That is not enough time to finish a job and return the call. Every missed call is a coin flip: either they wait, or they booked someone else before you put your camera down.

**Transition line into demo:**
"So let me show you the system I built. It runs on Raj Photo Video right now, and it fires every time a call goes unanswered."

---

## Section 3 — Workflow / Demo (30 seconds – 6 minutes)

**System to demo:**
rajphotovideo-email-automation — the missed call text-back automation. Trigger is a missed inbound call on the business phone. Action is an automated SMS response within 30 seconds, followed by lead notification to Sameer.

**Key steps to show:**

- Step 1: Incoming call is missed on the business phone — show the missed call notification
- Step 2: Automation fires — prospect receives a text message within 30 seconds: "Hey, it's Sameer from Raj Photo Video. I'm on-site and can't answer right now. What's your wedding date and estimated budget? I'll get back to you tonight."
- Step 3: Prospect replies with their details. Response is logged. Sameer receives a notification with the lead's name, date, and budget — ready to follow up personally that evening.

**Screen recording plan:**

1. Show the automation workflow: open the tool, display the trigger (missed call webhook) and the action (send SMS template)
2. Show a real example text exchange — blur the prospect's name and phone number, keep the message content visible
3. Show the lead notification Sameer receives — the summary card with date, budget, and follow-up status

**What NOT to show:**
Any prospect's full name, unblurred phone number, or personal details in the text exchange. Blur before recording. Do not show API keys, webhook tokens, or tool login credentials.

**CTA placement (mid-video, ~60% mark):**
"If this is something you're dealing with in your business, stick around — I'll show you the numbers in a second."

## Section 4 — Results (1-2 minutes)

**Primary metric:**
3 jobs recovered in the first month after the automation went live. At average wedding photography prices of $2,500-3,500 per booking, that is $7,500-$10,500 in recovered revenue — from a system that cost under $50 per month to run.

**Secondary metric:**
Response time went from "whenever I got off the shoot" — often 3-6 hours — to 30 seconds. That is the difference between the lead waiting for Sameer and the lead booking the next photographer on Google.

**Before / After framing:**

- Before: Called while shooting. Missed it. Called back in 3 hours. "Oh, we already booked someone, thanks."
- After: Missed call triggers immediate text. Prospect replies with budget and date. Sameer follows up that evening with a personalized message. Booked.

**How to present the number:**
"In the first month, three jobs came in through that text-back that would have gone to someone else. Three bookings at wedding photography prices. From a system that cost under $50 a month to run. That's not a productivity experiment — that's a business decision."

---

## Section 5 — Framework / Key takeaway (1-2 minutes)

**Core principle:**
Every business has a response-window problem. The business that responds first wins a disproportionate share of inbound leads. Automation does not replace the relationship — it protects the relationship from dying before it starts.

**The one-liner:**
"Automation doesn't replace you. It makes sure your name is still in the conversation by the time you can pick up the phone."

**Action step:**
Think about the last 3 times you missed a call from a new lead. What happened? If you do not know, that is the problem. If you do know, you can fix it. Start there — not with software, not with a system. Just with the question: what happens right now when someone calls and nobody answers?
