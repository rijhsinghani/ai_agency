# Video 1: Prep Talking Points

Talking points for the origin story video. Organized by section. Speak naturally — these are anchors, not a script.

---

## Section 1: Hook (0-10 seconds)

**The one line that opens the video:**
"I missed a call during a wedding shoot. By the time I called back, they'd already booked someone else. So I built a system that texts back in 30 seconds. Recovered 3 jobs the first month."

**Key points:**

- Lead with the LOSS, not the solution
- The frustration is universal — every service business owner has this story
- 30 seconds, not "instant" — specific numbers build credibility
- 3 jobs = real money, not hypothetical

---

## Section 2: Context (10-30 seconds)

**Who this is for:**

- Any business where you answer your own phone: photographers, plumbers, HVAC techs, electricians, contractors, cleaning companies
- The moment a call goes to voicemail, 70% of callers move to the next name on Google
- The window is under 5 minutes — research shows 21x better close rate at 5-minute response vs 30+ minutes

**The pain point to name directly:**
"You already know this problem. You've watched leads go cold because you were on a job. You can't answer the phone when you're elbow-deep in a water heater or shooting a ceremony."

**What NOT to say:**

- Don't use "AI-powered" or "platform" — say "system" or "automation"
- Don't say "SMB" — say "small business owner"
- Don't mention any employer or financial services background

---

## Section 3: Workflow Demo (30 seconds - 6 minutes)

**Walk through the screen-share using Diagram 2:**

1. "Here's what happens. A call comes in while I'm shooting."
2. "I can't answer. The system detects the missed call."
3. "Within 30 seconds, an automatic text goes out." (Show the text message)
4. "It says something simple: 'Hey, sorry I missed your call. How can I help?'"
5. "The prospect replies with what they need — a date, a budget, a question."
6. "I get a notification on my phone. Now I have their details and they're still warm."
7. "When I'm free, I follow up personally. By then, I'm not cold-calling. I'm continuing a conversation."

**Key technical points (keep non-technical):**

- Don't show API keys or credentials
- Don't explain Twilio or Make.com by name — just show what happens
- Focus on the OUTCOME: "30 seconds from missed call to engaged lead"
- Show a real text exchange with blurred names/numbers

**The bridge statement:**
"This is not complicated. The hard part isn't the technology. The hard part is knowing it exists and that it costs $200 a month, not $2,000."

---

## Section 4: Results (1-2 minutes)

**Switch to Diagram 3 (Before vs After) on screen:**

**Primary stat — say this clearly and slowly:**
"3 jobs recovered in the first month. At $1,400 average booking, that's $4,200 in revenue from a system that costs $200 a month."

**Secondary stats:**

- Response time: 4+ hours down to 30 seconds
- Weekly admin: 6-8 hours down to less than 1 hour
- Deposit follow-up: automated at 48 hours and 96 hours — 100% consistency
- Late-night inquiries: immediate response at 11 PM, weekends — no more "I'll get back to them tomorrow"

**The confidence line:**
"I built this for my own business first. Then I realized every service business has the same problem."

**What NOT to say:**

- Don't hedge — "roughly" or "approximately" weakens it
- Don't say "results may vary" — you're sharing what happened, not making a guarantee
- Don't compare to competitors by name

---

## Section 5: Framework / Takeaway (1-2 minutes)

**The one-liner for retention (memorize this):**
"Automation doesn't replace you. It makes sure your name is still in the conversation by the time you can pick up the phone."

**The framework to share:**
"Here's how I think about what to automate. Ask yourself three questions:

1. Is this happening repeatedly? Not once a year — every week.
2. Is it costing you money when it doesn't happen? Missed calls cost jobs. Missed follow-ups cost closes.
3. Can a system do it faster than you? If the answer is yes to all three, that's where you start."

**The action step:**
"Think about the last 3 calls you missed this week. What happened to those leads? Did they book? Did they call back? Or did they call the next person on Google? That's where automation starts."

**Show Diagram 4 (full automation stack) briefly:**
"This is what the full picture looks like for a service business. Missed call text-back is just the first piece. Lead follow-up, quote follow-up, appointment reminders, review requests — each one solves a specific revenue leak."
