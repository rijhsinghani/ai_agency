# Hero headline candidates — sameerautomations.com v2

**Generated:** 2026-04-12
**Constraint:** Brand voice — direct, peer-level, confident, practical, transparent. No buzzwords. No banned phrases. Sentence case. Numerals for metrics. Rotate industries. Sameer is the person, the brand is him.

**How to review:** Pick one number. You can mix the subhead from one option with the H1 from another — call it out if you do. All five options can be built; they test different hero angles.

---

## Option 1 — The 60-second promise (speed angle)

**Eyebrow:** `[ AUTOMATION SYSTEMS · BUILT BY SAMEER ]`

**H1:** Every lead gets a response in 60 seconds.

**Subhead:** Built custom for your business. Running in days, not quarters. I help small business owners — dental practices, roofers, salons, law firms, cleaning crews — stop losing jobs to whoever called back first.

**Primary CTA:** Book a call · **Ghost CTA:** See what I build

**Why this works:** Leads with the single hardest promise in the product. Speed is the one thing small business owners feel bleeding money every day. It is the exact number from case study 01 (4-hour average → 60 seconds). No guru language, no noun stacks. The subhead names 5 industries in one line, locking the "any industry" brand rule inside the hero.

## Option 2 — The money angle (dollars recovered)

**Eyebrow:** `[ AUTOMATIONS FOR SMALL SERVICE BUSINESSES ]`

**H1:** Recover $4,200 a month from leads you are already losing.

**Subhead:** I build the missed-call text-backs, quote follow-ups, and review sequences that keep money from slipping out of your business while you are on the job. Same systems I built for my own.

**Primary CTA:** Book a call · **Ghost CTA:** See the systems

**Why this works:** Opens with a real dollar figure from an actual client (wedding photography case, reframed as "event services" to avoid photo-only framing). Forces the visitor to think in ROI immediately. "Same systems I built for my own" closes with origin-story credibility without naming anything. The three automations in the subhead are the highest-ROI ones in the catalog.

## Option 3 — The origin-story angle (peer credibility)

**Eyebrow:** `[ SMART SYSTEMS · BUILT BY SAMEER ]`

**H1:** I built this for my own business first.

**Subhead:** Missed call text-back. Instant lead response. Quote follow-up. Review requests. All the systems I needed to stop losing jobs — now running for dental offices, roofers, salons, gyms, and cleaning crews across industries.

**Primary CTA:** Book a call · **Ghost CTA:** See the systems

**Why this works:** This is the peer-advisor angle at full volume. It frames Sameer as "the guy who solved this first, then helped others" — which is the exact brand persona from voice §1. Shortest H1 of the five, highest visual impact on a big Roc Grotesk display. The subhead rotates 5 industries explicitly. Best option if the goal is trust over speed.

## Option 4 — The problem-first angle (pain recognition)

**Eyebrow:** `[ YOU ARE BUSY · THE PHONE IS RINGING ]`

**H1:** You cannot answer the phone from a roof.

**Subhead:** Or a chair. Or a shoot. Or a house call. I build the systems that respond in 60 seconds while you are doing the actual job — for roofers, dental practices, salons, photographers, and any small business where the owner is the service.

**Primary CTA:** Book a call · **Ghost CTA:** See what I build

**Why this works:** Strongest hook of the five. Directly quotes a real Reddit post from case study 02 ("you cannot answer the phone from a crawl space"). Immediately signals "I understand your week" — peer-level voice at full strength. The H1 is short enough to run at full display weight without wrapping on desktop. The subhead does the industry rotation and the value prop in one breath.

## Option 5 — The outcome-first angle (transformation stated plainly)

**Eyebrow:** `[ LESS ADMIN · MORE BOOKED JOBS ]`

**H1:** Smart systems that run your back office while you run the job.

**Subhead:** Missed calls answered in 30 seconds. Quotes followed up automatically. Reviews requested at the right moment. Built custom for your business — whether that is a dental practice, a gym, a law firm, a cleaning company, or anything else where the owner is the product.

**Primary CTA:** Book a call · **Ghost CTA:** See the systems

**Why this works:** Closest to the brand tagline ("Smart systems. Built for your business. By Sameer."). Calmest of the five — lower-urgency, higher-transformation. Best for visitors who already know they want automation and are comparing providers. Weakest hook if the visitor is cold. Subhead has 5 distinct industries and names 3 automations with real mechanics.

## Claude's recommendation (non-binding)

**Option 1 for the H1, Option 4's eyebrow for personality.**

Reason: Option 1's "Every lead gets a response in 60 seconds" is the locked hero promise from the PRD (`snazzy-sauteeing-pixel.md` §positioning). Changing that is a PRD change, not a copy change. But Option 4's eyebrow (`[ YOU ARE BUSY · THE PHONE IS RINGING ]`) brings the peer-level voice into the top 8% of the page where it matters most. Combined: keep the PRD-locked H1, adopt the stronger eyebrow from Option 4, use Option 2's subhead as the fallback if you want to lead with dollars instead.

If you want to depart from the PRD promise, Option 4's H1 is the strongest departure. It is the most quoted sentence in the case studies and the most peer-level H1 in the set.
