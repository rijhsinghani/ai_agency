# Section architecture v2: AI growth partner

Research date: 2026-04-12
Previous: 9-section "automation shop" layout
New positioning: Full-service AI growth partner for small businesses

---

## Revised section order (11 sections)

### 1. Hero

Dark background (#1A1A1A or #0F172A). Full-viewport height. AuroraHero-style animated gradient (purple to cyan) with dot-grid overlay and grain texture. Cycling headline with industry words swapping every 3 seconds. Single CTA: "See what we build" (scrolls to services). Secondary CTA: "Book a call." Subtle particle or path animation in background -- continuous, not looping.

No logo wall in the hero. No "trusted by" yet. Let the headline land first.

---

### 2. Problem statement (the gap)

3-column layout on dark background. Each column states a pain point with a metric:

- **No brand presence.** "73% of consumers judge a business by its website before ever calling."
- **No follow-up system.** "40% of inbound leads go cold because nobody responds in time."
- **No content strategy.** "Businesses posting 3x/week get 2.5x more inbound leads than those posting monthly."

Short closer line: "You know the problem. You have been living it. Here is what changes."

This replaces the old "problem/agitation" section with broader scope -- not just missed calls, but the full digital presence gap.

---

### 3. Services grid

Card-based grid on dark background with glow accents. 5 service cards, each with a signature accent color and icon:

| Service                    | Accent color             | One-line description                                                                              |
| -------------------------- | ------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Brand and digital presence | Primary purple (#7B2FBE) | Logo, website, and digital identity that makes you look like you mean business                    |
| Email marketing automation | Cyan (#4DD9E8)           | Sequences that nurture leads, confirm bookings, and ask for reviews -- without you writing a word |
| Content creation           | Warm orange (#D97706)    | Social posts, video clips, and written content produced on a schedule you set                     |
| Lead capture and follow-up | Success green (#10B981)  | Missed call text-back, form responses, and follow-up sequences that run 24/7                      |
| Review and reputation      | Light purple (#9B4FDE)   | Automated review requests after every job, with monitoring and response                           |

Each card expands on hover/tap to show 2-3 specific deliverables. Inspired by Together AI's modular card layout.

---

### 4. Transformation stories

Not "case studies" -- transformation stories. Each one follows the arc:

**Before state** (specific, painful) --> **What we built** (systems, not features) --> **After state** (metrics)

Example structure:

- "A dental office with zero online reviews and a website from 2014" --> "New brand, automated review requests, weekly content" --> "147 Google reviews, 3x website traffic, fully booked 6 weeks out"

3 stories minimum, rotating industries (dental, cleaning, photography). Full-width cards with before/after metrics displayed as large numerals. Scroll-triggered reveal animation.

This replaces the old "$4,200 recovered" single-metric proof with broader transformation narratives.

---

### 5. How it works (the process)

Horizontal timeline or numbered accordion (inspired by Lambda's 01-04 pattern):

1. **Discovery call.** 30 minutes. I learn your business, your bottlenecks, and what you have tried. No pitch.
2. **Growth audit.** I map every gap -- brand, website, follow-up, content, reviews. You get the audit whether or not you hire me.
3. **System build.** 2-4 weeks depending on scope. Real systems, not templates. You see progress weekly.
4. **Launch and optimize.** Systems go live. I monitor, adjust, and maintain on retainer. You focus on your business.

Clean, scannable, no jargon. Each step has a subtle animation on scroll entry.

---

### 6. Technology preview

Dark section with animated node graph or pipeline visualization. Shows the actual technology stack visually -- not as a feature list, but as a living system diagram. Nodes for: AI content generation, automated email sequences, CRM integration, review monitoring, lead routing, analytics dashboard.

Lines connect the nodes with the purple-to-cyan gradient (matching the logo's "input/output bridge" motif). Subtle pulse animation on the connections to suggest data flowing.

This section answers "how is this different from hiring a marketing agency" without saying it explicitly. The visual makes the AI/automation layer tangible.

---

### 7. Origin story

Split layout: text on the left, system architecture visualization on the right.

"I ran a photography business. I was on shoots 12 hours a day and losing leads every time I could not answer the phone. So I built the systems myself -- automated follow-up, review requests, content scheduling, the works. It worked. Then other business owners started asking me to build the same thing for them. That is what Sameer Automations is."

Short. First person. Peer-level. No hero framing. The visualization on the right shows a simplified version of the actual systems (reinforcing "I use what I sell").

---

### 8. Social proof bar

Logo wall + 2-3 pull quotes. Not a full testimonial section -- that is coming. Just enough to build trust mid-page before the pricing section. Logos of businesses served (with permission) or industry icons representing served verticals.

---

### 9. Pricing / engagement model

Transparent pricing section. Not "schedule a call to learn more" -- that violates brand voice.

Two tiers:

- **Growth starter.** One core system (e.g., lead follow-up OR review management). Monthly retainer. Price range displayed.
- **Full growth partner.** All five services. Monthly retainer. Price range displayed.

Each tier lists what is included. "Retainer" is used (per brand terminology). Honest about what the retainer covers: maintenance, updates, optimization, support.

---

### 10. FAQ

6-8 questions, accordion style. Address the real objections:

- "What industries do you work with?" (Any service business where the owner is busy with customers)
- "How long until I see results?" (Specific timelines per service)
- "What if I already have a website?" (Audit it, improve it or rebuild it)
- "Do I need to understand AI?" (No. You need to understand your business. I handle the systems.)
- "What happens if I cancel?" (You keep what was built. Systems are yours.)
- "How is this different from hiring a marketing agency?" (I build systems that run without me. Agencies sell hours.)

---

### 11. Final CTA

Full-width dark section with a single bold headline and one button. No distractions.

"Ready to stop losing business to competitors who show up online and you do not?"

Button: "Book a free growth audit"

## Design principles (extracted from research)

1. **One signature interaction, fully committed.** The best futuristic sites pick one interactive moment (cycling headline, animated pipeline, 3D element) and let it carry the page. Do not scatter effects.

2. **Dark theme + glow accents = instant technical credibility.** Every top-performing AI site in 2026 uses dark backgrounds with luminous accent colors. Our purple-to-cyan gradient is already built for this.

3. **Metrics in the first viewport, story in the scroll.** Lead with a specific number or transformation, not a vague promise. The narrative unfolds as the visitor scrolls, but the hook is immediate and quantified.
