**Section 1 — Hook (0-10 seconds)**:
Problem: A potential client called while Sameer was on a shoot. Nobody answered. By the time he called back, they had booked someone else.
Opening line (use the exact hook from content-plan.md): "I used to lose jobs. Not because my work wasn't good. Because a potential client called while I was shooting and nobody picked up. By the time I called back, they'd booked someone else. I was frustrated. So I built something that would text them back immediately, explain why I couldn't answer, and ask for their budget and date. I recovered 3 jobs in the first month. Then I realized every small business owner had a version of the same problem."

**Section 2 — Context (10-30 seconds)**:
Who this affects: Any service business owner who takes calls while they are working — photographers, plumbers, contractors, HVAC techs. If you are on-site, you cannot answer the phone, and the person who called will call the next person on Google.
Why it matters: The window to respond to an inbound inquiry is under 5 minutes before the prospect moves on. That is not enough time to finish a job and return the call.

**Section 3 — Workflow / Demo (30 seconds – 6 minutes)**:
System to demo: rajphotovideo-email-automation — the missed call text-back. Show the actual automation trigger and response sequence.
Key steps to show:

- Step 1: Incoming call is missed on the business phone
- Step 2: Automation fires — prospect receives a text message within 30 seconds: "Hey, it's Sameer from Raj Photo Video. I'm on-site and can't answer right now. What's your wedding date and estimated budget? I'll get back to you tonight."
- Step 3: Prospect replies. Response is logged. Sameer gets a notification with the lead's details.
  Screen recording plan: Show the automation workflow (trigger + action), show a real example text exchange (blur identifying details if needed), show the lead notification.
  Mid-video CTA (at the point where you reveal the result — approximately 60% through): "If you want to talk about building something like this for your business, I'll leave a link in the description to book a free 15-minute call. No pitch — just a conversation about what's costing you time."

**Section 4 — Results (1-2 minutes)**:
Primary metric: 3 jobs recovered in the first month after the automation went live. At average wedding photography prices of ~$2,500-3,500 per booking, that is $7,500-$10,500 in recovered revenue — from a system that cost under $50/month to run.
Secondary metric: Response time went from "whenever I got off the shoot" (often 3-6 hours) to 30 seconds.
Before/After framing:

- Before: Called while shooting. Missed it. Called back in 3 hours. "Oh, we already booked someone, thanks."
- After: Missed call triggers immediate text. Prospect replies with budget and date. Sameer follows up that evening with a personalized message. Booked.

**Section 5 — Framework / Key takeaway (1-2 minutes)**:
Core principle: Every business has a response-window problem. The business that responds first wins a disproportionate share of inbound leads. Automation does not replace the relationship — it protects the relationship from dying before it starts.
Action step: Think about the last 3 times you missed a call from a new lead. What happened? If you do not know, that is the problem. If you do know, you can fix it.

**Section 6 — Call to action (10-15 seconds)**:
Closing line (verbatim, per brand voice rules): "If you want to figure out what's costing you leads in your business, book a free 15-minute call. Link is in the description. No pitch, just a conversation."
