**Structure per thread (6 tweets):**

1. HOOK (pattern interrupt) — Provocative dollar-math statement that stops the scroll. e.g., "You're paying $20,800/year for a problem that takes 30 seconds to fix." Do NOT open with "I". Start with the reader's reality or a number.
2. BEFORE/COMMON MISTAKE — What the broken manual process looks like. Vivid and specific to the industry. e.g., "You're in the chair with a client. Phone rings. You ignore it. That caller dials the next number."
3. THE MATH — Multiply the small loss by time. Surface the annual number. "5 missed calls a week. $80 each. That's $400/week walking out the door. $20,800 a year."
4. THE FIX — Specific enough to be credible. Name the tools. "A Twilio workflow sends a text within 30 seconds: 'Caught your call — tied up with a client. Can I reach you in an hour?'"
5. PROOF/TRANSFORMATION — Before/after result or community evidence. Reference real data from the guides. "Manual follow-up converts at 5%. Automated converts at 40%."
6. CTA — Guide URL + keyword CTA + booking link: "Full breakdown: sameerautomations.com/guides/{slug}.html — DM me {KEYWORD} for the walkthrough. Or grab 15 min: https://calendar.app.google/psycao3CrXjGnmk48"
