# Custom Build — Discovery Call Intake Template

**Use for:** Prospects whose workflow doesn't fit a standard package, or who want to combine multiple automations into an integrated system.

**Call length:** 45 minutes
**Outcome:** Enough information to scope the build and deliver a fixed-price quote within 24 hours.

**Price range to set expectations:** $2,500–$5,000 build + retainer TBD based on complexity

---

## Before the Call

Review any notes from the lead's inquiry form or prior conversation. Identify:

- Which standard package(s) they were considering (if any)
- Their industry and approximate business size
- Any specific pain points they mentioned

Have this document open during the call. Take notes in each section as you go.

---

## Section 1: Current Workflow (Understand the Problem)

Goal: map exactly what happens today, manually, step by step.

**Questions to ask:**

1. Walk me through what happens from the moment [trigger event] occurs to the moment [desired outcome] is complete. What does that look like today, step by step?

2. Who does this work right now — you, a staff member, or does it fall through the cracks?

3. How long does it take each time? How often does it happen per day or per week?

4. What happens when it doesn't get done, or when there's a delay? What's the real cost of that?

5. Have you tried to fix this before — a VA, a different tool, a different process? What happened?

## Section 2: Triggers (What Starts the Automation)

Goal: identify the specific event or condition that kicks off the workflow.

**Questions to ask:**

1. What's the very first thing that needs to happen for this workflow to start? (A form submission? A phone call? A job marked complete? A date or time? A payment received?)

2. Where does that event happen — which tool or system registers it? (Your website, your CRM, your scheduling app, your email, a spreadsheet?)

3. Is the trigger always the same, or are there variations? (e.g., "sometimes it's a web form, sometimes it's a walk-in call")

4. How frequently does this trigger fire? Give me a realistic estimate — per day, per week, per month.

5. Are there conditions that should prevent the automation from running even if the trigger fires? (e.g., "only for jobs above a certain value" or "only for new customers, not returning ones")

## Section 3: Data Sources (What Information Is Needed)

Goal: understand what data the automation needs to access and where it lives.

**Questions to ask:**

1. What information does the person or system receiving the output need to know? (Name, job type, address, quote amount, appointment time, phone number — list everything.)

2. Where does each piece of that information currently live? (A form field, your CRM, a spreadsheet, your scheduling app, your accounting software?)

3. Is any of this information scattered — like some of it's in one place and some in another?

4. Do you have a CRM or customer database? If yes, which one and how long have you been using it?

5. Are there any tools you're currently using that you absolutely want to keep? (This helps us understand the integration constraints.)

## Section 4: Desired Output (What Should Happen as a Result)

Goal: define exactly what the automation needs to produce — a message sent, a record created, a notification triggered, a task assigned.

**Questions to ask:**

1. At the end of this automation, what should have happened? What's the output — a text message, an email, a calendar event, an updated record, a Slack message, something else?

2. Who receives it — a customer, a staff member, you, or multiple people?

3. Does the output need to be personalized? If so, with what information? (First name, job details, dollar amount, appointment time?)

4. What channel should it go through — SMS, email, both? Do you have a preference for tone (professional, conversational)?

5. Is there a follow-up step — does something need to happen if there's no response after a certain amount of time?

6. Are there different outputs depending on what the prospect or customer does? (e.g., "if they reply, do X; if they don't reply in 48 hours, do Y")

## Section 5: Success Metric (How Will We Know It's Working)

Goal: establish a clear, measurable definition of success before the build begins. This becomes the acceptance criteria.

**Questions to ask:**

1. How would you know, three months from now, that this automation was worth doing? What would have changed?

2. Is there a number you're currently tracking that you'd expect to improve — close rate, no-show rate, review count, response time, hours saved per week?

3. What's the current baseline? (e.g., "right now we close 20% of quotes" or "right now we spend 2 hours a day on this")

4. What improvement would make this obviously worth it to you?

5. Is there a threshold below which it wouldn't be worth continuing? (This helps size the retainer conversation — if the ROI is marginal, a simpler solution may serve them better.)

## Section 6: Logistics & Constraints

These questions take 5 minutes but prevent problems during build.

**Questions to ask:**

1. Who on your team will be the main point of contact during the build? Who approves the final version before go-live?

2. Do you have someone technical in-house, or is it just you? (Helps calibrate the handoff doc and training level.)

3. Are there any data privacy or compliance considerations? (Healthcare? Financial services? Specific state regulations about SMS marketing?)

4. Do you have existing accounts for any of the tools we'd likely use — Twilio for SMS, a calendar tool, an email sending platform?

5. What's your timeline? Is there a specific date you need this running by?

6. Budget range: are you comfortable in the $2,500–$5,000 range, or do you have a ceiling I should know about?

## Post-Call: Scoping Checklist

Before writing the quote, confirm you have answers to all of the following:

- [ ] Trigger event identified and confirmed (what starts the workflow)
- [ ] Source system for the trigger identified (which tool registers it)
- [ ] All required data fields identified (what info the automation needs)
- [ ] All source systems for data identified (where that info lives)
- [ ] Output defined (what is produced, for whom, via which channel)
- [ ] Branching logic documented (if X then Y, if no response then Z)
- [ ] Success metric and baseline agreed upon
- [ ] No compliance blockers identified (or flagged for further review)
- [ ] Timeline and budget range confirmed
- [ ] Contact person confirmed

**Deliver quote within 24 hours.** Quote should include: scope summary in plain English, what's included, what's excluded, build price, monthly retainer, estimated timeline, and payment terms.
