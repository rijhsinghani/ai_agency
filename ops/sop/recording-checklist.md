# Recording Checklist

> Use this checklist for every video. Complete pre-shoot the day before or morning of filming.
> Mark items done with `[x]`. Leave notes inline if something was adjusted.

---

## Pre-Shoot (day before or morning of)

- [ ] Outline is complete — every section filled in, opening line rehearsed out loud at least once
- [ ] Screen recording software is open and tested (OBS, QuickTime, or equivalent) — record 10 seconds and confirm it captures correctly
- [ ] Portfolio piece / demo system is logged in and working — manually trigger the workflow before filming to confirm it fires
- [ ] Background is clean and consistent with previous videos (same location, same setup, no new items in frame)
- [ ] Lighting is adequate — face is visible, no harsh shadows across eyes, no window backlight washing out the image
- [ ] Audio tested — record 10 seconds, play back with headphones, check for hum, echo, or background noise
- [ ] Microphone positioned correctly — 6-10 inches from mouth, off-axis from any fans, AC units, or vents
- [ ] Water on desk (keep it off camera — move before filming)
- [ ] Phone set to Do Not Disturb — all notifications silenced
- [ ] Camera / screen resolution confirmed: 1080p minimum, 4K preferred (crop flexibility in post-production)
- [ ] Backup recording option identified — phone or second device available if primary capture fails

---

## During Shoot

- [ ] State the video title or topic out loud before the first take — helps locate the real take in editing
- [ ] Film the hook a minimum of 3 times — pick the best take in editing, not on set
- [ ] Pause 2-3 seconds between sections — gives clean edit points without jarring cuts
- [ ] Speak to one specific person (the business owner in the problem statement), not a generic audience
- [ ] Deliver the mid-video CTA on camera: "If you want help with this, I have a link below to book a free 15-minute call"
- [ ] Film the demo / screen recording separately from talking-head segments — do not combine in one take
- [ ] Record a transition line before switching to screen: "Let me show you what that looks like"
- [ ] Record a transition line when returning from screen: "So that's the system — here's what it actually did"
- [ ] End screen: look directly at camera, slow down, deliver CTA clearly — do not rush the last 15 seconds
- [ ] Record a backup hook — a second version of the opening in case the first take doesn't feel right in review
- [ ] Check in-camera preview after the first hook take — confirm framing and exposure before continuing

---

## Wrap

- [ ] Review footage on device before breaking down the setup — confirm hook, demo, results, and CTA segments are all captured
- [ ] Verify screen recording files are present and playable (separate from talking-head files)
- [ ] Export raw files to organized folder: `content/videos/raw/[video-slug]/`
- [ ] Label files clearly: `hook-take-1.mp4`, `hook-take-2.mp4`, `demo-screen.mp4`, `results-segment.mp4`, `cta-segment.mp4`
- [ ] Back up raw footage to external drive or cloud storage before starting any edits
- [ ] Log total recording time and any notes about what needs re-shooting: `content/videos/raw/[video-slug]/notes.md`
