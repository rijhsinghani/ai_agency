# Day 7 — Value-Add Email

**Purpose:** This is the final touch in the 3-message sequence. Deliver something real — a personalized Loom video that walks through their specific business. The goal is to make it so easy and low-stakes to watch that even a skeptical prospect clicks.

**Send timing:** 7 days after Day 1. Set the calendar reminder when you send Day 1.

**Requirement:** You must have completed the free audit for this business before sending Day 7. The Loom video is the audit, on screen. If you haven't done the audit, do it before sending this message.

**Effort level:** This is the most labor-intensive message in the sequence. One personalized Loom per prospect. Target: 10 per week once the system is running.

---

## Subject Line

```
Quick video for [Business Name]
```

Short and specific. "Quick" signals it won't take long. "[Business Name]" signals it's not a mass email.

## The Loom Video Format

**Length:** 3 minutes. Not 2 minutes, not 5 minutes. 3. Business owners will watch 3 minutes. They won't watch 5.

**What to record:** A screen recording of you going through their business from a customer's perspective. Show their actual website, their actual Google profile, and the actual audit findings. No slides. No stock footage. Just their real business on your screen.

**Structure (3 minutes, timed):**

**0:00 - 0:20 — Open with their name and business**
"Hey [First Name], this is Sameer. I spent about 20 minutes looking at [Business Name] from a customer's perspective — what happens when someone finds you and tries to reach out. I found three things worth flagging, and I want to walk you through them. This is going to take about 3 minutes."

**0:20 - 1:10 — Gap 1 (show it on screen)**
Pull up the actual evidence. If Gap 1 is slow response time, show the contact form on their website. If it's missing after-hours contact, show the page where there's no alternative to "call us." Narrate what a customer experiences.
"So the first thing I noticed is [gap description]. Here's what a customer sees when they land here at 9 PM and try to reach you..."

**1:10 - 2:00 — Gap 2 (show it on screen)**
Pull up their Google Business Profile if the gap is there. Read an actual review aloud if it mentions response time. Make it real.
"The second thing is [gap description]. I pulled up your Google page, and here's what stood out..."

**2:00 - 2:40 — Gap 3 (show it on screen)**
Same format. Screen share the evidence.
"And the third one is [gap description]. When I tested [the specific action — submitting a form, sending a Google Maps message], here's what happened..."

**2:40 - 3:00 — Close with the CTA**
"These three things together are probably costing you somewhere between [low estimate] and [high estimate] in missed leads per month. I'm happy to jump on a 30-minute call to walk you through exactly what I'd do to fix each one. The link to book is in the email. No obligation."

## Loom Setup Notes

**Tool:** Loom (loom.com). Free tier allows unlimited videos. No account required for the viewer to watch — they just click the link.

**Recording:** Use Loom's Chrome extension or desktop app. Select "Screen + Cam" so your face appears in the corner while you're sharing their website. The face-in-corner makes it feel like a personal message, not a recording.

**Settings:** Set the video to "Anyone with the link can view" — do not require sign-in. Any friction and they won't watch.

**Practice run:** Before sending to a real prospect, record one practice Loom on a business you are not going to reach out to. Watch it back. You're looking for: does it feel personal? Did you stay under 3 minutes? Is the screen share clear and easy to follow? Fix what feels off before you go live.

## After Day 7 — What Happens Next

**No response after Day 7:** The sequence is complete. Mark them "Sequence Complete — No Response" in Notion. Do not contact them again for at least 60 days. After 60 days, if they're still a strong prospect, you can restart the sequence with a fresh angle.

**Response after Day 7:** Work the conversation. Offer the 30-minute call. Send the written audit document if they want to review it before committing to a call.

**They watched the Loom but didn't reply:** Loom shows you who watched and how long they watched. If someone watched the full 3 minutes but didn't reply, send one more message: "Hey [First Name] — saw you had a chance to watch the video. Happy to answer any questions, or jump on a quick call if you'd like to go further. No pressure either way." This is the only time you add a 4th message to the sequence.
