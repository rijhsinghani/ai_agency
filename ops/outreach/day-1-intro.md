# Day 1 — Introduction Email

**Purpose:** First contact. The goal is not to sell. The goal is to earn a reply by showing you've actually looked at their business and found something real.

**Research requirement:** Spend 10-15 minutes on this prospect before writing. Look at their Google reviews, their website, and their Google Business Profile. The opening observation must be specific — if it could apply to any business, rewrite it.

**Volume:** Send a maximum of 5 per day so you can respond quickly to any replies.

---

## Subject Line Options (A/B Test)

Pick one per prospect. Rotate between them and track which generates more opens.

- `Quick question about [Business Name]'s follow-up process`
- `Found something on [Business Name]'s Google page`
- `[First Name] — noticed something about your reviews`

---

## Full Email Template

```
Hi [First Name],

I came across [Business Name] while looking at [City] [business type]s on Google.

[PERSONALIZED OBSERVATION — choose the option that matches what you actually found. Delete the others.]

--- OPTION A: Use this if you found a review mentioning slow response, hard to reach, or not returning calls ---

I noticed a few of your reviews mention response time — one from [month] says "[brief quote from an actual review]." That's the kind of thing that costs a service business real revenue, and it's fixable.

--- OPTION B: Use this if their website has no way to contact them after hours or no booking option ---

I checked out your website and noticed there's no way for someone to book or leave their info after hours. In a business like yours, that's probably costing you leads every week.

--- OPTION C: Use this if you didn't find a specific signal but they fit the profile ---

For most [plumbers/contractors/dentists] in [City], the biggest revenue leak isn't marketing — it's leads that call or contact you and never hear back within a few minutes. That gap is where competitors win.

---

I build systems that automatically follow up every lead within 60 seconds — even at 2 AM — qualify them, and book appointments without you touching anything.

I did a quick review of [Business Name]'s setup. I found a few specific things that are likely costing you leads right now.

Would it be useful if I put together a short breakdown? No cost, no pitch — just honest observations.

— Sameer
sameer@rajphotovideo.com
```

---

## Personalization Notes

**[First Name]:** Owner's first name. Find it on the website About page, Google Business Profile, or LinkedIn. If you can't find a name, use "Hi there" — but try harder first. A name matters.

**[Business Name]:** Exact business name as it appears on Google.

**[City]:** The city or neighborhood where they operate. Keep it specific — "South Austin" is more personal than "Austin."

**[business type]:** Match how they describe themselves. "plumber" not "plumbing professional." "dentist" not "dental care provider."

**[Personalized observation]:** This is the most important sentence in the email. It tells them you actually looked. Generic phrases like "businesses like yours" signal that you didn't. If you can't find anything specific after 10 minutes of research, move to the next prospect and come back to this one.

---

## LinkedIn / Instagram DM Version

Use this for social media outreach. Keep it short — DMs are skimmed, not read.

```
Hi [First Name] — I came across [Business Name] and noticed [specific observation in one sentence].

I help [business type]s in [City] automatically follow up every lead and book appointments without lifting a finger.

I took a quick look at your setup and found a couple of things worth flagging. Would a short breakdown be useful? No cost.

— Sameer
```

**DM tip:** On LinkedIn, connect first with a short note — "Hi [First Name], I work with local [business type]s in [City] on lead follow-up. Thought it might be worth connecting." Wait for acceptance, then send the DM. Cold DMs without a connection request get ignored.

---

## What Success Looks Like

A response — any response, including "not interested" — means your targeting and message are working. A reply rate of 10-20% across 50 outreach messages is solid. If you're below that after 20 messages, review the personalization. The observation is probably too generic.
