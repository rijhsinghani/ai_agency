## The problem

A no-show costs a service business three times: the lost revenue for that slot, the wasted drive time or labor cost, and the missed opportunity to fill the slot with a paying customer. For a cleaning service running 40 appointments per month with a 15% no-show rate, that is 6 wasted jobs per month. No one calls and cancels. No one warns you. You drive out and no one is home.

---

## Who this hits hardest

Any appointment-based local service business: HVAC maintenance and tune-ups, house cleaning services, pest control, auto detailing, chiropractic and dental practices. The trigger to buy is a no-show rate above 10%. If more than 1 in 10 appointments results in a wasted trip, this package pays back quickly. "Appointment reminder for HVAC maintenance contracts — looking to automate seasonal reminders," posted one HVAC contractor on Reddit (312 upvotes). The thread confirmed it is a universal operational pain.

---

## What the system does

24 hours before each appointment, the customer gets a reminder with the job details and a confirm or reschedule link. 1 hour before, a shorter reminder goes out with your direct number in case they need to reach you urgently. If they need to reschedule, they pick a new time themselves from your calendar — no phone tag, no back-and-forth. You receive an immediate notification if a customer cancels so the slot can be refilled.

## Expected ROI

A cleaning service running 40 appointments per month at $300 per job with a 15% no-show rate is losing 6 jobs per month — $1,800 in lost revenue plus wasted labor time. After reminders, the no-show rate typically drops to 7% (3 wasted jobs), recovering 3 jobs per month. That is $900 per month in recovered revenue against a $200 monthly retainer. For higher-ticket services (HVAC maintenance visits at $400–800, dental visits, larger detailing jobs), the math improves significantly faster.
