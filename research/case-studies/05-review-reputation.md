## The problem

Most satisfied customers do not leave reviews — not because they were unhappy, but because nobody asked at the right moment. The window is narrow: 2 hours after a job is done, when the customer is still happy and the work is still on their mind. Miss that window and the review does not happen. "I have 31 Google reviews and my competitor has 180 — how did they get so many?" posted one HVAC owner on Reddit (523 upvotes). The answer is almost always the same: they ask every customer, automatically, at the right time.

---

## Who this hits hardest

Any local service business where Google reviews drive new customer decisions: HVAC companies, plumbers, landscapers, cleaning services, auto repair shops. If your Google Business Profile has fewer than 50 reviews or your rating is below 4.5 stars, every additional review has outsized impact on how often Google surfaces your listing.

---

## What the system does

Two hours after a job is marked complete in your scheduling app, the customer gets a personalized text: "Thanks for choosing us today — if you had a great experience, it would mean a lot if you left us a quick Google review." One tap opens your review page directly. No searching, no friction. Satisfied customers do it if you make it easy. An optional 3-day follow-up goes to customers who meant to do it but forgot.
