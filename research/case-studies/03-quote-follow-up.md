## What the system does

When a quote is sent and logged, a 3-touch sequence fires automatically over 10 days.

**Day 2:** A friendly check-in: "Just following up on the quote I sent for [job type] — wanted to make sure you got it and see if you have any questions."

**Day 5:** A practical offer: "Happy to answer anything before you decide. What would be most helpful?"

**Day 10:** A gentle nudge: "The quote is set to expire soon — I can lock in that price if you're ready to move forward."

Each message is personalized with the prospect's name, the job type, and the quote amount — pulled automatically from your records. They read like you wrote them, not like a mass text.

The moment the prospect replies, books, or is marked as closed or dropped, the sequence stops. No awkward follow-up after they have already said yes. No messages to someone who told you they went another direction.

You also get a live view of every open quote: where it is in the follow-up sequence, whether the prospect engaged, and whether it closed. Never wonder again how many quotes are still in play.

## What made this work

The follow-up gap is not laziness. It is a capacity problem. When you are running jobs, managing your crew, and fielding new calls, "circle back on the Hendersons quote" slips off the priority list. By day 5, you are not sure if they decided. By day 10, calling feels awkward.

Automation removes the awkwardness. The system sends a message on day 2 because that is when it is supposed to — not because you remembered to do it. There is no social friction, no internal debate about whether it is too soon or too late.

The data from real business owners confirms this. The entrepreneur who shared his close rate improvement on Reddit did not change his pricing. He did not get better at the work. He just followed up consistently, and his close rate went from 23% to 41% in 60 days.

A lawn care owner posted a similar pattern: "losing customers because I don't follow up after quotes." That is the whole problem, stated plainly. The fix is not more willpower. It is a system that follows up for you every time.

The other factor: personalization matters. A message with your name, the prospect's name, and the specific job type feels like a direct communication, not a marketing blast. Prospects who get a personalized follow-up on day 2 are far more likely to respond than prospects who get a generic reminder a week later.
