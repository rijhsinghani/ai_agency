## Client profile

Solo wedding and event photographer running a 1–2 person operation. Peak season runs May through October. The owner is the shooter, the salesperson, and the admin — all at once.

During a shoot, nobody is answering the phone. During an edit session that runs until midnight, nobody is chasing deposits. The business lives and dies on the owner's ability to respond fast and follow through consistently. Before automation, that wasn't happening.

---

## Before state

Here's what a typical week looked like before any systems were in place:

- **Missed calls went unreturned for 4+ hours.** When a potential client called during a shoot and hit voicemail, the odds they were still available 4 hours later — and hadn't booked someone else — were low.
- **Lead follow-up was manual and inconsistent.** After an inquiry came in, following up was a task on a mental list. On busy weeks, that list didn't get worked.
- **Deposit chasing happened on the owner's time.** After sending a booking agreement, the follow-up to collect the deposit was manual: a text, then another text, then a phone call. Each one was 5 minutes the owner didn't have.
- **Booking confirmations went out during business hours only.** If someone signed and paid at 11 PM on a Saturday, they didn't hear back until Monday. That silence creates doubt.
- **Weekly admin block: 6–8 hours.** Between responding to inquiries, following up on quotes, chasing deposits, and sending booking confirmations, the owner was spending 6 to 8 hours every week on tasks that weren't photography.

---

## The problem

The business was losing 3 bookings per month to response time alone.

Not to price. Not to competition on quality. To the 4-hour gap between when a lead called and when the phone got answered.

In the wedding photography market, couples are often comparing 3 to 5 photographers simultaneously. They submit inquiries on a Friday night or a Sunday afternoon — exactly when a working photographer is on a job or recovering from one. The first photographer to respond with something useful gets the advantage. The one who calls back Monday morning is often the third or fourth name on a list that's already narrowing.

At $1,400 per booking (average), 3 lost jobs per month is $4,200 in revenue that never showed up.

The owner knew leads were slipping. There was no way to track exactly how many, but the pattern was clear: couples who got a response within an hour almost always booked a call. Couples who got a response the next day almost never did.

---

## The solution

The system built here has 3 components that work together:

### 1. Missed call text-back

The moment a call goes unanswered, the caller gets a text within 30 seconds:

> "Hey, caught your call — I'm in the middle of a shoot right now. Can you tell me your wedding date and what you're looking for? I'll get back to you as soon as I wrap up."

This does two things. It signals that the business is real and responsive. And it gives the lead something to do instead of immediately calling the next photographer on the list.

Most people reply. The lead stays warm.

### 2. Automated lead follow-up sequence

When a new inquiry comes in via the contact form, an automated sequence fires:

- **Within 60 seconds:** A personalized text confirming receipt of the inquiry, with the photographer's availability calendar linked so the lead can book a consultation call directly.
- **Day 2 (if no booking):** A follow-up text: "Still have your date open — would love to connect and see if we're a good fit."
- **Day 5 (if still no booking):** A final nudge with a direct question about their timeline.

If the lead books at any point, the sequence stops. No awkward follow-up after they've already said yes.

### 3. Booking confirmation + deposit follow-up

When a lead books a consultation and then signs a contract:

- An automated confirmation goes out within 60 seconds, regardless of what time it is. Someone who signs at 11 PM gets a confirmation at 11 PM.
- If the deposit isn't collected within 48 hours, an automated reminder fires: "Just a heads-up — your booking isn't locked in until the deposit is received. Here's the link."
- A second reminder fires at 96 hours if the deposit is still outstanding.

The owner no longer spends mental energy tracking which deposits have come in and which haven't. The system handles it.

## What made this work

The system isn't complicated. The missed call text-back is a single-trigger automation — call goes unanswered, text fires. The follow-up sequence is 3 messages over 5 days with a stop condition. The deposit reminder is a time-based trigger after a contract is signed.

None of these are technically difficult. What's hard is remembering to do them manually, consistently, every time, when you're also the person shooting 8-hour weddings on the weekends.

The automation doesn't do anything the owner couldn't do themselves. It does it every time, immediately, without requiring the owner to remember.

That's the point.

The other thing that mattered: the messages read like the owner wrote them. They're not robotic confirmation emails. They're short, warm, and specific to the conversation. A lead who gets a text at 11 AM on a Saturday that says "caught your call — in a shoot right now, tell me your date" doesn't think they're talking to a bot. They think they're talking to someone who cares enough to respond immediately.

That's what closes the gap.
